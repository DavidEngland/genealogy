== Biography ==

'''Sarah Tapley Clark''' was born on 23 February 1806 in Tennessee, United States. She was commonly recorded as "Sally" or "Aunt Sallie" in local records. At the time of her birth, her father [[Clark-14234|Robert Clark]] was 28 years old and her mother [[Farmer-1267|Lucy Farmer]] was 22 years old.

On 27 March 1829, Sarah married [[Duncan-164|Moses Duncan]] in Lauderdale, Alabama, United States.

== Children ==

Sarah and Moses Duncan had at least four sons and five daughters. According to the 1850 Federal Census for Lauderdale County and subsequent death records, their children included:

* '''Sarah Duncan''' (b. ca. 1830) m John Coates Johnson
* '''George Washington Duncan''' (b. ca. 1832; d. 1917) m Sarah Maria Smith
* '''Martha E. Duncan''' (b. 25 July 1832) – married [[Whitten-500|Jesse P. Whitten]] (1827–1882)
* '''Robert Duncan''' (b. ca. 1834)
* '''Mary Duncan''' (b. ca. 1836) m Elias Brown
* '''Elmira (Almira) Duncan''' (b. ca. 1840) m William C. Carothers
* '''Thomas J. Duncan''' (b. ca. 1842; d. 1932)
* '''James Andrew Duncan''' (b. ca. 1845; d. 1926) m Annie Flynt

Her children with Moses Duncan were further connected to prominent local families through their marriages to members of the Whitten, Parish, Brown, Carothers, and Johnson families.

Sarah died on 6 June 1894 in Lauderdale, Alabama, United States, at the age of 88 years, and was buried in Lauderdale, Alabama, United States.

== Children's Death Records ==

Sarah is named as the mother in the following Alabama death records for her children:

* '''George Washington Duncan''' (died 1917): Sarah Clark listed as mother
* '''James Andrew Duncan''' (died 1926): Sarah Clark listed as mother; Moses Duncan listed as father
* '''Thomas J. Duncan''' (died 1932): Sarah Clark listed as mother; Moses Duncan listed as father

== Biography Notes ==

Sarah's middle name is recorded in historical documents as '''Tapley''' or '''Tarpley'''. Both spellings appear in various census records and marriage documents from the era. Her nickname "Sally" was commonly used in local records and on census enumerations, alongside the more formal designation "Aunt Sallie" in family and community documents.

The 1850 Federal Census (Lauderdale County, household #408) lists her as '''Sarah Duncan''', age 44, born in Tennessee, living with her husband Moses Duncan (age 43, born in South Carolina).
