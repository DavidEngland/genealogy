== Biography ==

David Adkisson Jr. was born on October 22, 1796, in Halifax, Halifax County, Virginia, United States, to David Adkisson Sr. (age 38) and Emily Elizabeth Birdsey Yerby (age 46). He grew up in Virginia before eventually settling in Tennessee.

=== Marriage and Family ===

On January 27, 1816, in Roane, Tennessee, United States, David married Bridget Drusilla Foshee. (Note: Some historical records list this marriage date as January 29, 1816; the discrepancy likely reflects variations in historical documentation rather than an error.) Together they were the parents of at least 5 sons and 4 daughters, establishing a substantial family in the Tennessee frontier.

=== Residences ===

David spent approximately 40 years living in Lawrence County, Tennessee, establishing deep roots in the community. By 1880, census records show him residing in District 1, Grainger County, Tennessee, United States.

=== Death ===

David Adkisson Jr. died on December 13, 1886, in Wayland Springs, Lawrence County, Tennessee, United States, at the advanced age of 90 years.

=== Research Notes ===

David Adkisson Jr. had a daughter, Sarah A. Adkinson, who married Thomas Anderson McDonald on June 24, 1852, in Lawrence County, Tennessee. Through this connection, the Adkisson family is linked to the McDonald family of Lauderdale County, Alabama.
