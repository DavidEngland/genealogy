== Biography ==

'''William T. Pigg''' was born on 17 March 1842 in Wayne County, Tennessee, United States to [[Pigg-85|John Henry Pigg I]] (1817–1879) and [[Gray-5898|Eliza Jane (Gray) Pigg]] (1817–1896). His father was 25 and his mother was 25 at the time of his birth.

He married [[Scott|Mary Jane (Scott) Pigg]] (1842-1907) on 31 October 1861 in Wayne County, Tennessee, United States.<ref>"Tennessee, Marriages, 1796-1950," index, ''FamilySearch'' (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/XDQB-39S : accessed 12 Oct 2013), W. T. Pigg and Mary J. Scott, 31 Oct 1861.</ref>

They were the parents of at least 7 sons and 6 daughters:
* [[Pigg|Jasper Newton Pigg]] (1862-1906)
* [[Pigg|John Henry Pigg]] (1864-1888)
* [[Pigg|Frederick Marion Pigg]] (1865-1916)
* [[Pigg|Susan C Pigg]] (1867-1868)
* [[Pigg|McDougal Pigg]] (1868-1869)
* [[Pigg|Mary C Pigg]] (1870-1920)
* [[Pigg|William M Pigg]] (1872-1953)
* [[Pigg|James D Pigg]] (1873-1874)
* [[Pigg|Sarah J Pigg]] (1875-1951)
* [[Pigg|Elvira J Pigg]] (1877-1955)
* [[Pigg|Ellen Pigg]] (1878-1959)
* [[Pigg|Eliza Ann Pigg]] (1881-1969)
* [[Pigg|Delaware Pigg]] (1884-1932)

In 1860, at the age of 18, his occupation was listed as farm laborer in Wayne County, Tennessee, United States.

William was a Free Mason.

=== Death ===

William died on 22 February 1888 in Wayne County, Tennessee, United States, at the age of 45, and was buried in Lawson Cemetery, Wayne, Tennessee, United States.
