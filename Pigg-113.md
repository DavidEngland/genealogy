== Biography ==

Delaware Pigg was born on 27 February 1883 in Tennessee, United States, the son of William T. Pigg and Mary Jane Scott. He lived in Civil District 5, Wayne, Tennessee, United States for about ten years and in Civil District 5, Claiborne, Tennessee, United States in 1930. He died on 20 January 1932 in Lutts, Wayne, Tennessee, United States, at the age of 48, and was buried in Lawson Cemetery, Wayne, Tennessee, United States.

=== Marriage ===
He married Minnie Ida Darby on 3 January 1909 in Wayne, Tennessee, United States. They were the parents of at least two sons and two daughters. He later married [[Throgmorton-8|Buleah Mary (Throgmorton) McCord]] on 27 June 1931 in Wayne County, Tennessee, United States.

=== Death ===

Delaware Pigg died on 20 January 1932 in Lutts, Wayne, Tennessee, United States, at the age of 48. He was shot and instantly killed by Deputy Sheriff A. B. Gillis during an arrest attempt at the Pigg farm. According to newspaper reports, Gillis went to arrest one of Pigg's sons on a theft charge. When Pigg attempted to shoot the deputy, his gun jammed, and Gillis opened fire. Pigg's son then shot the deputy in retaliation before fleeing in an automobile. Gillis was wounded but survived and was taken to hospital for treatment.<ref>"One Killed, Deputy Wounded in Duel," The Chattanooga News (Tennessee), Thursday, January 21, 1932, p. 1.</ref>
