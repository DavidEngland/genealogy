== Biography ==
'''[[Lewis-8883|Ruth Whitley Lewis]]''' was born approximately '''1780''' in '''Lincoln County, Kentucky'''. She was the daughter of '''[[Lewis-9841|JosephFrancis Lewis]]''', a skilled brickmason originally from England or Ireland, and '''[[Whitley-753|Sarah Whitley]]'''. Through her mother, Ruth was the niece of the famed Kentucky pioneer '''[[Whitley-757|William Chapman Whitley]]''', who built the first brick house west of the Blue Ridge Mountains and was renowned as an Indian fighter and frontiersman.
== Marriage ==
Ruth married '''[[Hargrove-277|Valentine Hargrove]]''' on '''9 October1799''' in '''Lincoln County, Kentucky'''. The marriage was witnessedby '''Samuel Lewis''' and '''James Hardgrove'''. Valentine was born around '''1778''' in '''Amherst, Virginia''', and was an early settleron the Kentucky frontier with strong connections to the influential Whitley family through his marriage to Ruth.
== Family Narratives ==
Family tradition records that Valentine Hargrove traveled with his brother-in-law '''William Whitley''' to Cherokee towns to secure the release of white prisoners taken during frontier conflicts. During one such peace mission, the party was threatened by warriors. Valentine reportedly demonstrated such resolve when asked if he could fight that theIndians complimented him as a '''"good warrior"''' and permitted the group to return home with several prisoners.

== Migration and Later Life ==
Following their marriage, Ruth and Valentine eventually migrated southward into the Tennessee-Alabama borderlands. Valentine established hishome in '''Hardin County, Tennessee'''. The family's migration southward represents the broader pattern of frontier settlement expansion during the early 19th century.
The lineage of Ruth and Valentine Hargrove proved particularly significant to the history of the Shoals area through their son '''[[Hargrove-287|James Monroe Hargrove]]''' and his daughter '''Lydia Serena Hargrove''', whose marriage to '''James Ervin White''' linked the family to the '''Whitten''' and '''England''' family lines.
== Death ==
Ruth is believed to have passed away before '''1840''' in '''Hardin County, Tennessee'''. Valentine died between '''1851 and 1860''', also in '''Hardin County, Tennessee'''.
