== Biography ==
'''Sarah (Whitley) Lewis''' (abt. 1752 – 21 April 1837) was part ofthe early settlement of the Kentucky frontier. Born in Augusta County,Virginia, she was the daughter of '''[[Whitley-754|Solomon Whitley]]''' and '''[[Barnett-2252|Elizabeth Barnett]]''' and the sister of '''[[Whitley-757|William Chapman Whitley]]''', the famed Kentucky pioneercredited with building the first brick house west of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
== Early Life and Marriage ==
Sarah married '''[[Lewis-9841|Joseph Francis Lewis]]''' on '''2 January 1779''' in '''Rock Castle, Rockbridge County, Virginia'''. Joseph was a skilled brickmason from England or Ireland. The couple later established their home in Rock Castle County (now Rockcastle County), Kentucky, placing Sarah within a network of influential Kentucky pioneers and frontiersmen of the Bluegrass region.
Sarah's siblings included '''[[Whitley-749|Margaret Whitley]]''', '''[[Whitley-752|Ruth (Whitley) Collier]]''', '''[[Whitley-757|William Chapman Whitley]]''', '''[[Whitley-750|Mary Whitley]]''', '''[[Whitley-748|James Whitley]]''', '''[[Whitley-755|Solomon Whitley Jr.]]''', '''[[Whitley-747|Catherine Whitley]]''', and '''[[Whitley-756|Thomas Whitley]]'''.

== Faith and Family Legacy ==
Sarah’s family heritage was marked by deep religious conviction; herfather Solomon was noted for teaching the old Presbyterian catechismto his grandchildren at Whitley’s Station. Sarah’s descendants continued the family’s pattern of westward movement and public serviceacross the expanding American frontier.
== Death ==
Sarah died on 21 April 1837 in Burkesville, Cumberland County, Kentucky, surviving her husband by approximately a decade.
