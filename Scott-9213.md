## Biography

'''John Martin B. Scott''' (1791–1870) was a prominent '''Magistrate''' in Wayne County, Tennessee, known for his service on the county court and his establishment of a large family on 48 Mile Creek in the Western Highland Rim region.

=== Early Life and Migration ===

John Martin B. Scott was born in '''1791''' in '''Virginia''', the first child of '''James Scott''' (b. 1765, North Carolina) and '''Rebecca''' (b. abt. 1776, North Carolina). The family first settled in '''Williamson County, Tennessee''', prior to 1820, then moved to '''Wayne County, Tennessee''', sometime before 1820.

John Martin filed for a '''Revolutionary War Tract of 200 acres''' on '''48 Mile Creek''', which became the family homestead.

=== Marriages and Family ===

==== First Marriage ====

John Martin married '''Anna Elizabeth''' (maiden name likely '''Carrell''' or '''Snapp''') around '''1822'''. Anna Elizabeth died about '''1829'''.

'''Children with Anna Elizabeth:'''
* '''[[Scott-XXXX|Samuel E. Scott]]''' (Rev. Samuel Scott) (b. March 6, 1823, Wayne County, Tennessee; d. 1905), married Lydia Keeton

==== Second Marriage ====

John Martin married '''[[White-16150|Mary Cynthia White]]''' (1813–1890) about '''1829''' in '''Wayne County, Tennessee'''. Mary Cynthia was the daughter of '''Thomas White''' (or possibly George White) and '''Mewborn''' (surname variant). Together they had at least eight sons and four daughters:

'''Children with Mary Cynthia White:'''
* '''[[Scott-7959|Frederick Marion Scott]]''' (b. 1832)
* '''[[Scott-7956|John James Scott]]''' (b. February 10, 1838; d. 1926)
* '''[[Scott-7957|William P. M. Scott]]''' (b. May 12, 1839; d. 1915), married '''[[Pigg-XXXX|Sarah Jane Pigg]]''' in 1862
* '''[[Scott-7950|Mary Jane (Scott) Pigg]]''' (b. December 23, 1842; d. September 1907), married '''[[Pigg-XXXX|William T. Pigg]]''' in October 1861
* '''[[Scott-7955|Madison Columbus Scott]]''' (b. November 1846), married '''[[Gray-XXXX|Elizabeth A. Gray]]''' in March 1870
* '''[[Scott-7954|Eliza (Scott) Anderson]]''' (b. 1848; died young)
* '''[[Scott-7961|Franklin Pierce Scott]]''' (b. 1849; d. October 1921), married '''[[Cady-XXXX|Elvisa Cady]]''' in June 1876, moved to McNairy County, Tennessee
* '''[[Scott-7960|Isabella Scott]]''' (b. 1851; listed as "Israel" on 1860 census), married '''[[Gray-XXXX|George W. Gray]]''' in September 1868

=== Public Service ===

John Martin Scott served as a '''Magistrate''' in Wayne County, a position of considerable civic responsibility on the county court. On '''November 18, 1844''', he deeded a sorrel mare to his nephew '''William P. Jackson''', son of Nancy.

=== Death and Burial ===

John Martin Scott died about '''1870''' in '''Wayne County, Tennessee''', at approximately age 79. He is buried at '''Jackson Cemetery''', Collinwood, Wayne County, Tennessee.<ref>Find A Grave Memorial #29766127</ref>

His widow, '''Mary Cynthia Scott''', lived with her youngest son '''[[Scott-7961|Franklin Pierce Scott]]''' in the 1880 census. She died in '''1890''' in '''McNairy County, Tennessee''', and is buried there near the family of Franklin. A location on '''48 Mile Creek''' known as '''"Granny Scott Hollow"''' is reportedly named in her honor.

=== Context: Early Scott Families in Wayne County ===

The Scott family was part of a regional social ecosystem of pioneering families including the Coopers, Simses, Lindseys, Robersons, Nowlins, Gallahers, Parkers, Dickersons, Davis, Thompsons, Yates, and Walkers who settled the area around 48 Mile Creek.

'''Early Scott Names in 1820 Wayne County Census:'''
Berry, James, Jeremiah, John, Joseph, Martin, William F, Adams Scott

'''Early Scott Names in 1830 Wayne County Census:'''
James, James Sr., John, John Sr., John M, Rosamond P

'''1849-1850 Wayne County Court Records''' document various Scott family members in administrative and civic roles during this period.
