===Tax Lists, Prince Edward county===
Henry Pigg appeared in the 1782 tax list with 3 horses and 10 cattle. His tax was 8 s. 6 d.<ref>Prince Edward, Virginia, "Personal property tax lists, 1782-1850," p. image #117, Henry Pigg; digital images, Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, ''FamilySearch'' (www.familysearch.org : accessed 17 Jul 2018); citing 1782 tax list, FHL microfilm 1905691, item 2: "Personal property tax lists 1782-1809 (No lists for 1808)".</ref> In 1783 his name appeared as Henry Pig. He still had 10 cattle, but now had 4 horses.<ref>Prince Edward, Virginia, "Personal property tax lists, 1782-1850," p. image #131, Henry Pig; digital images, Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, ''FamilySearch'' (www.familysearch.org : accessed 17 Jul 2018); citing 1783 tax list, FHL microfilm 1905691, item 2: "Personal property tax lists 1782-1809 (No lists for 1808)".</ref> In 1784, his horses and cattle remain the same, but he is now paying for Wm Pigg in addition to himself, recorded for the first time as being over 16.<ref>Prince Edward, Virginia, "Personal property tax lists, 1782-1850," p. image #144, Henry Pigg William Pigg; digital images, Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, ''FamilySearch'' (www.familysearch.org : accessed 17 Jul 2018); citing 1784 tax list, FHL microfilm 1905691, item 2: "Personal property tax lists 1782-1809 (No lists for 1808)".</ref>

Henry Pigg appeared in the 1790 tax list, recorded on 22 Mar. He paid tax on 2 "blacks above 16" and 5 horses. His tax was Â£1 10s.<ref name="1790 tax list">Prince Edward, Virginia, "Personal property tax lists, 1782-1850," p. image #301, Henry Pigg; digital images, Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, ''FamilySearch'' (www.familysearch.org : accessed 17 Jul 2018); citing 1790 tax list, District of Charles Allen, commissioner, FHL microfilm 1905691, item 2: "Personal property tax lists 1782-1809 (No lists for 1808)".</ref> He was recorded again exactly one year later, on 22 Mar 1791. For the first time, he also paid for Valentine Pigg, who must therefore have just turned 16. The rest of his "property" was the same.<ref name="1791 tax list">Prince Edward, Virginia, "Personal property tax lists, 1782-1850," p. image #328, Henry Pigg; digital images, Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, ''FamilySearch'' (www.familysearch.org : accessed 17 Jul 2018); citing 1791 tax list, District of Charles Allen, commissioner, FHL microfilm 1905691, item 2: "Personal property tax lists 1782-1809 (No lists for 1808)".</ref>

===Slaves===
In both 1790 and 1791, Henry Pigg paid taxes on 2 "blacks above 16." Thus far, neither of their names have been revealed.<ref name="1790 tax list" /><ref name="1791 tax list" />
