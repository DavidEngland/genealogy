== Biography ==

'''Joseph Francis Lewis''' (c. 1750-1756 – c. 1825-1828) was a brickmason and early American settler who emigrated from England or Ireland to establish himself in Kentucky during the late 18th century. He is notable for his marriage into the prominent Whitley family and his skilled trade as one of the early brickmasons in the Kentucky frontier, most famously for constructing the first brick house west of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

==== The Whitley Connection ====

William Whitley, Joseph's brother-in-law, was a Revolutionary War veteran and renowned frontiersman who built the first brick house west of the Blue Ridge Mountains. Known as the **"Sportsman's Hill"** or **Whitley House**, this structure was completed around 1787-1794 near present-day Stanford, Kentucky, and still stands today as a historic landmark. The house served as a fort during Indian conflicts and was a gathering place for early Kentucky settlers.

Given Joseph Lewis's profession as a skilled brickmason and his close family connection to William Whitley, it is quite possible that Joseph contributed his expertise to the construction of this historic brick house or other early brick structures in the region. The Whitley house was remarkable for its time, featuring:

==== Payment for the Brickwork ====

The '''Lincoln County Deed Books''' provide physical evidence of the transaction between Whitley and Lewis. On '''October 21, 1805''', Whitley executed a '''land deed''' to '''Joseph F. Lewis''' for '''228 acres''' of his land in exchange for a consideration of '''£328'''.

The Filson Club History Quarterly notes that if this land transfer constituted full payment for the masonry, the brickwork would have cost roughly '''$1,500''', described as "no mean sum for that day". This substantial payment in land reflects both the value of Joseph's skilled work and the prosperity of the Whitley family.

According to legend recorded in the quarterly, a German man named '''Henry Clark''' furnished the whiskey for the workmen during construction and was also reportedly paid in land, though deed records show a 1804 sale to a Henry Sheven rather than Clark.

=== Trade and Occupation ===

Joseph Lewis was a **brickmason** by trade, a valuable and specialized skill on the Kentucky frontier where most construction consisted of log cabins. Brickmasons were essential craftsmen who:

* Required years of training and apprenticeship
* Possessed knowledge of mortar mixing, brick laying, and structural design
* Were among the more prosperous artisan class in early America
* Contributed to the permanence and development of frontier settlements

His skills would have been in high demand for constructing:
* Chimneys and fireplaces for log homes
* Brick houses for wealthy settlers
* Public buildings and churches
* Foundation work and other masonry projects

The presence of a skilled brickmason like Joseph Lewis in early Kentucky represents the transition from temporary frontier settlements to more permanent communities.

=== Historical Context ===

Joseph Lewis lived during a transformative period in American history:
* The late Colonial period and American Revolution (his youth)
* The settlement of Kentucky and the frontier wars (1770s-1790s)
* Kentucky statehood (1792)
* The War of 1812
* The westward expansion into Missouri and beyond

As a skilled craftsman, he represented the artisan class that was essential to transforming frontier settlements into established communities. His work as a brickmason provided the literal foundation for permanent settlement in Kentucky.
