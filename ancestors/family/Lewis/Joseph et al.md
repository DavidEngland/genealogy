To update WikiTree with the most accurate information based on the provided sources, here are the biographies for **Joseph Francis Lewis**, his parents **Joseph Lewis** and **Winney King**, and his known siblings.

### **Joseph Lewis (The Father)**
**Joseph Lewis** was a prominent member of the "western aristocracy" in colonial Virginia, residing in **Goochland and Henrico Counties**. He was a man of significant social standing and civic influence, as evidenced by his extensive service in **Henrico Parish (St. John’s Church)**.
*   **Civic Service:** He is documented in the *Vestry Book of Henrico Parish* as a **"Gentleman Vestryman"** from 1752 through 1773. He was repeatedly elected to the prestigious office of **Church Warden** (serving terms in 1753, 1754, 1755, 1757, 1758, and 1764) and was appointed the **Parish Collector** in 1766.
*   **Marriage:** He married **Winney King**, a connection confirmed by research in the *Annals of Henrico Parish* (Microfilm F3652).
*   **Family Standing:** His position in the Virginia Tidewater hierarchy provided the "genealogical capital" that allowed his children to transition successfully to the Kentucky frontier.

### **Winney King (The Mother)**
**Winney King** was the wife of **Joseph Lewis of Goochland**.
*   **Kentucky Records:** Evidence of her lineage appears in Kentucky legal records; her granddaughter **Ruth Whitley Lewis** and Ruth's husband, **Valentine Hargrove**, were named as legatees in a **King estate** settlement.
*   **Business Ties:** Her son, **Joseph Francis Lewis**, is also recorded as being in business with members of the King family in Kentucky, further cementing her maternal link to that family.

### **Joseph Francis Lewis (The Son)**
**Joseph Francis Lewis** (c. 1750–c. 1825) was a master artisan who brought permanent architectural structures to the Kentucky wilderness.
*   **Origin:** Contrary to some immigrant narratives, he was a native of **Goochland/Henrico, Virginia**, and the son of Joseph Lewis and Winney King.
*   **Trade and Legacy:** A skilled **brickmason**, he is most famous for building **"Sportsman’s Hill,"** the first brick house west of the Allegheny Mountains, for his brother-in-law, **Colonel William Whitley**. In October 1805, Whitley deeded him **228 acres** of land, likely as payment for his expert masonry work.
*   **Marriage:** He married **Sarah Whitley** (c. 1752–1837) around 1780. Sarah was the sister of the noted Indian fighter William Whitley.
*   **Children:** His children included **Ruth Whitley Lewis** (who married Valentine Hargrove), Sarah, Isabella, Mary A., **Samuel** (who followed his father's trade as a brickmason), Joseph, William, and Benjamin.

### **Known Siblings of Joseph Francis Lewis**
Joseph Francis Lewis had at least two siblings who were also part of the Virginia elite:
1.  **Sarah Lewis:** She married **Milner Redford**. Her daughter married William Robards, and her grandson, **Lewis Robards**, was the first husband of **Rachel Donelson**, who famously married President **Andrew Jackson** as her second husband.
2.  **Colonel John Lewis:** He married **Mildred Lewis**, the daughter of **Colonel Robert Lewis** and **Jane Meriwether**. This marriage linked the family to the prominent **Warner Hall** lineage, which included ancestors of **George Washington**.
