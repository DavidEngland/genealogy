RUTH WHITLEY LEWIS
Ruth Whitley Lewis was the wife of Valentine Hargrove. Her father was Joseph Francis Lewis. Her grand father was Joseph Lewis of Goochland who married Winney King (This information discovered recently in a Microfilm rented from A.G.L.L., F3652 "Annals of Henrico Parish.")
Ruth's father was the brother of Sarah Lewis who m. Milner Redford their dau. m. Wm Robards, their son Lewis Robards m. Rachel Donaldson who m. 2nd President Andrew Jackson.
Ruth's father was the brother of Col. John Lewis that m. Mildred Lewis. Mildred Lewis was named for her father's maternal grandmother Mildred Reade Warner. Mildred's father was Col. Robert Lewis and wife Jane Meriwether.
Col. Robert Lewis's parents were John Lewis and Elizabeth Warner. Elizabeth Warner's sister Mildred Warner m. Augustine Washngton. Augustiine Washington's son Augustine m. as his 2nd wife Mary Ball and they were the parents of George Washinggton. Elizabeth and Miildred's father was Col. Augustine Warner Jr. and wife Mildred Reade.
Mildred Reade's father was Col. George Reade of York Co. VA. and wife Elizabeth Martiau. Elizabeth Martiau parents were Capt. Nicholas Martiau and wife Jane Berkley.
The Reade, Washington and Martiau families are in the heritage records of England showing them back to King John and nine of the Barons off the Magne Carta. They go from King John to William the Conquerer and Charlemagne.
Joseph Francis Lewis is shown in Ky. in business with a King. Valentine and Ruth were shown as legatees in a King estate. (These deeds are in the Sources section of this book, Ky record) Winney King was Ruth Whitley Lewis's grandmother.
Colonel William Whitley was Ruth's Uncle, her mother's brother. He was a famous Indian fighter. He along with his friends, Daniel Boone, John Hardgrove and brother in law General George Rogers Clarke built Crab Orchard Fort in KY The Lewis and Clarke were of the families, Lewis and Clarke discoverer's of the "Northwest Passage."

GEORGE HARGROVE
George Hrgrove patented 250 acres of land in Christianson Co. Ky. on the state waters of the Fort Donelelson Creek on Sept. 12, 1816. ( cert. %2427, found in the archives of Frankfort Ky.) On May 22, 1821 Gorge Hargrove gave his son, William B. Hargrove "For and in consideration of the love and goodwill he doth bear toward the said, William B. Hargrove 100 acres being part of a patent granted to the said George Hargrove... in the year of our Lord One Thousand Eight Hundred and Sixteen." (This found in deed Book A. pg. 120, Trigg Co., Ky)
George Hargrove was dead by 20 December 1823 when his heirs sold his 100 acres on Donelson Creek to Joseph Isbell ( Triggs Co. Ky. Deed Book A, pgs. 416,417) From this deed we know he had three sons: William B. Hargrove, Willis Hargrove, and John Hargrove.
William B, Hargrove m. Sarah (Sally) but from Court Order Book A. pg.349, Trigg Co. Ky., we have proof of William B. Hargrove's widow, Sarah (Sally) putting out for apprenticeship these children, "herein of William B. Hargrove, deceased." This Court Order was dated 21 Sept. 1829. The Children listed were: Jett (Jethro), Nathan, Nathaniel, and Alfred.
We have proof of Richard Hargrove being born 4 mos after William died.
