The sources provide extensive documentation of land grants and deeds for the **Hargrove**, **Whitley**, and **Lewis** families, reflecting their roles as prominent frontier landowners and military veterans.

### **Hargrove and Hargrave Land Records**
*   **Robert Hargroves** purchased **200 acres** in **Edgecombe County, North Carolina**, on the north side of the Tar River in 1749, later selling the same tract to George White in 1750.
*   **Dudley Hargrove** received **land grant #524** in **Halifax County, North Carolina**, for **49 acres** in 1792.
*   Following his migration to Alabama, **Dudley Hargrove** is recorded in the **Pickens County Tract Book** as owning two separate parcels consisting of **291 acres** and **168 acres**.
*   **Hezekiah Hargrove** entered an indenture in 1780 to purchase **306 acres** on Rucker’s Run in **Amherst County, Virginia**, from Martin King Jr. for £100.
*   **Willis Hargrove** and his wife Elizabeth sold land in **Bertie County, North Carolina**, to William Granberry in 1769 and later sold a tract to **James Swin Hargroves** in 1775.
*   **Davis Grisham** (Hargrove kin) was awarded **two bounty land warrants** in **Pope County, Illinois**, as compensation for his service in the War of 1812.

### **Whitley Family Grants and Claims**
*   **William Chapman Whitley** received a **military land grant** in Kentucky for his Revolutionary service.
*   In 1779, the Virginia land commission issued William a **1,400-acre certificate** for a settlement on **Cedar Creek** near the Crab Orchard.
*   In 1780, William was granted a **second 1,400-acre certificate** as the assignee of **Valentine Harmon**, which included the land surrounding his established station.
*   For his later service in the Northwest Campaign, William received a **private’s allotment of 108 acres** (divided into 100-acre and 8-acre tracts) in **George Rogers Clark’s military grant** on the north bank of the Ohio River.
*   As his sons reached their majority, William and Esther Whitley deeded **200 acres each** to **Solomon, William Jr., and Andrew Whitley** to establish them as farmers.
*   **Jonathan Whitley** was deeded **200 acres** on **Buffalo Creek** in Augusta County, Virginia, by Paul Whitley in 1750.
*   **Robert Whitley** was granted land in **Washington County, Virginia**, as the assignee of Richard Fields in 1804.

### **Lewis and Allied Family Deeds**
*   **Joseph Francis Lewis**, a skilled brickmason, was deeded **228 acres** of land by his brother-in-law **William Whitley** on October 21, 1805, for a consideration of £328.
*   Historical analysis suggests this substantial land transfer may have served as **payment for the professional brickwork** Joseph provided for the construction of Whitley’s historic home, **"Sportsman’s Hill"**.
*   **Samuel Lewis** (son of Joseph) inherited his father's trade and established his own land holdings in **St. Charles County, Missouri**, after migrating there in 1816.
*   **John Lewis** (of the Staunton line) was a prolific landowner who helped found **Augusta County** and became a wealthy developer of the **Shenandoah Valley**.
*   **Nicholas Perkins** was granted **3,000 acres** in the **Seventh District of Lawrence County, Tennessee**, in 1824 under a law intended to encourage the incorporation of **Iron Works**.
