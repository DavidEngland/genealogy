'''Death''': Nicholas  Hargrave died on April 23, 1534 in Wakefield Parish, Yorkshire, England. <ref>Source: [[#S82]] </ref> ''citation needed''
From "The Hargrove Family Study": YORKSHIRE PEDIGREES Hargrave of Warley in Sowerby and Wakefield: Nicholas Hargrave,. Yoeman of Sowerby andWakefield, Yorkshire. Will, 6 Feb 1531 Probate 23 Apr 1534. [Spouse]Agnes d. 1545/6; will dated 22 Feb 1545. [Children] Lawrence Hargrave,Richard Hargrave, Elizabeth Hargrave, and John Hargrave. Lawrence Hargrave will 26 Jan 1542/3 Probate 14 July 1543. Richard Hargrave, livedin Sowerby, will administered 22 Dec 1557. Had a son Richard II who lived in Sowerby. His will was administered  1583.
Another possible source that fits date and location wise: Quitclaim 1522 ...Nicholas Hargrave, smith, of Wakefeld,...All claims etc., to land in Wakefeld, Stavelay, Allerthorpe, Thornes and Sandal, which they had of Elizabeth and John Roberts, her husband, on 20th October, 1520.The National Archives Kew, Richmond: https://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details
This profile's uploaded transcription of the will of Nicholas named wife Agnes and daughters: Agnes, Isabel, Jenett, Margaret, Elizabeth, and Alice. Nicholas left instructions that he was to be buried in the church of All Hallows in Wakefield (Wakefield Cathedral was known as AllHallows up until the 16th century, and changed to All Saints after the reformation, which in England was soon after the date of this will).He left monetary gifts to All Hallows, Saint Johnâ€™s Church, Mary Magdalen Chapel, and "bequeath unto the 4 orders of friars whom of them5s, and who order to make me a dirge." Agnes was appointed executrix.Supervisors were William Roberts, John Michell, Oliver Addy, and William Watts.  Witnesses were Richard Carnabe, William Henning and WilliamBathus (and other men?).
