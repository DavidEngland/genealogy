= Biography =

'''James B Hartgrove''' (also known as '''Hargrove''') was born in 1776 in Amherst, Virginia, United States. He was the son of [[Hargrove-261|John Washington Hargrove Sr.]] (born 1752) and [[Ball-555|Mary (Ball) Hargrove]] (born 1748).

He married Sarah (Sally) Moore on 27 May 1801 in Pulaski, Kentucky, United States. James and Sally were buried just east of the present town of Somerset, Pulaski County, in the Patterson Cemetery on a farm known as the Schoolcraft Farm. James's tombstone has no dates, but Sally's shows she was born 24 August 1781 and died 7 August 1851.

James died before September 1834 in Pulaski, Kentucky, United States. After his death, Sally married Archibald Woods on 20 September 1834 and moved to Wayne County, Kentucky.

== Land Grants ==

* 24 July 1799: Granted 200 acres of land south of Green River on the Cumberland River
* 29 November 1815: Granted 13 acres on the Cumberland River

== Public Service ==

James was active in the formation of Pulaski County, serving as a commissioner and as the first sheriff, with Reban Payne as his deputy. He later worked as a surveyor.

In 1802, he served on the Board of Trustees for the Somerset Academy, the first school in Pulaski County.

In August 1805, he was paid 50 pence for being a witness in a two-day suit against Samuel Newell.

== Estate ==

James's will left his estate to his wife Sarah. Certain parcels of land that he had obtained from his brother [[Hargrove-277|Valentine]] were left to his niece Jane Patterson.

After James's death, Sally entered into contract with their nephew Lewis Patterson to operate the plantation, for which she was paid twenty dollars a month beginning in 1836.
