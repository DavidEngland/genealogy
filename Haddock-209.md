== Biography ==

'''James W. Haddock Jr.''' was born 2 February 1795 in North Carolina, United States and died 13 July 1876 in Wayne County, Tennessee. He was buried at Whitten Chapel Cemetery, Wayne County, Tennessee.

* [[Phillips-7361|Elizabeth Ann (Phillips) Haddock]], m. 3 March 1864 at her father's house in Lauderdale County, Alabama.

=== Military Service ===
James served as a Private in Captain Thomas WHITE's company, South Carolina Militia from 1 October 1814 to 7 March 1815. He volunteered on 1 September 1814 at Sipses Old Field, Union District, South Carolina, and was discharged at Charleston, South Carolina. He served as a substitute for draftee '''James Jackson of The Forks of Cypress''', Florence, in the Shoals of the Singing (Tennessee) River region.

According to his pension statement (No. 20.482), his command's march included: Charleston, SC → Headrells Point near Charleston → Bull's Island → St. John's Island → Charleston (discharge).

'''Military Land Warrants:'''

James received two bounty land warrants as compensation for his substitute service:
* Warrant No. 12.763 for 80 acres (1850)
* Additional warrant for 80 acres received 7 April 1856

These land grants facilitated his migration to Wayne County, Tennessee, where he settled near the Tennessee-Alabama border region.
