== Biography ==

William Jonas Adkisson was born on February 2, 1855, in Wayland Springs, Lawrence County, Tennessee, United States, to William Thomas Adkisson (age 28) and Susan Crews (age 26). He was the son of William Thomas Adkisson, who was the brother of Sarah A. Adkinson, wife of Thomas Anderson McDonald.

=== Marriage and Family ===

On August 6, 1879, in Wayne, Tennessee, United States, William Jonas married Leatha Jane Willis. Together they were the parents of at least 3 sons and 3 daughters.

=== Residences ===

William lived in Hardin County, Tennessee for approximately 5 years. Census records from 1900 show him residing in Civil District 4, Hardin, Tennessee, United States, at age 44, working as a farmer.

=== Death and Burial ===

William Jonas Adkisson died on December 18, 1940, in Savannah, Hardin County, Tennessee, United States, at the advanced age of 85 years. He was buried in Savannah Cemetery, Savannah, Hardin County, Tennessee.

=== Family Connections ===

William Jonas Adkisson was connected to the McDonald family through his uncle William Thomas Adkisson's marriage to Sarah A. Adkinson (who married Thomas Anderson McDonald on June 24, 1852, in Lawrence County, Tennessee). This connection links the Adkisson family to the McDonald and England families of Lauderdale County, Alabama.
