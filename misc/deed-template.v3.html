No. 131 - Spring Term

Commonwealth of Kentucky

In the Court of Appeals
April 30th, 1802
Absalom Bridges
Plaintiff in Error
vs.
Valentine Hargrove
Defendant

Upon a writ of error to reverse a judgment of the Court of Quarter Sessions of Pulaski County. In this cause, the following errors are insisted upon by the Counsel, to wit:

1st. That it is not averred that the defendant obtained a certificate for the land.
2nd. That it is not alleged the defendant had any title to convey.
3rd. That no special request is laid.

The Court deems it unnecessary to decide the first two, the third being fatal. The rule is well settled that where the defendant is chargeable on a collateral matter and not on a mere debt, there ought to be a request precisely alleged as to time and place. The general allegation that he has often been requested is not sufficient.

Wherefore, it is ordered and adjudged that the judgment aforesaid be reversed...

Judgment Reversed.
Teste: Achilles Sneed, C.C.A.
