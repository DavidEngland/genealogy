--> Bridges v. Hargrove (1802)

Commonwealth of Kentucky

Court of Appeals

Spring Term, 1802

Absalom Bridges,
Plaintiff in Error
v.
Valentine Hargrove,
Defendant
[ 2 Ky. 131; 1 Sneed 131 ]

Prior History. Upon a writ of error to reverse a judgment of the Court of Quarter Sessions of Pulaski County.

Date of Decision. April 30, 1802.

Opinion of the Court.

In this cause, the following errors are insisted upon, viz:

  1. That it is not averred that the defendant obtained a certificate for the land.
  2. That it is not alleged the defendant had any title to convey.
  3. That no special request is laid.

The Court deems it unnecessary to decide the first two, the third being fatal. The rule is well settled that where the defendant is chargeable on a collateral matter and not on a mere debt - as, for example, where a conveyance for a tract of land is to be made on demand - there ought to be a request precisely alleged as to time and place, and the general allegation that he has often been requested is not sufficient.

Wherefore, it is ordered and adjudged that the judgment aforesaid be reversed; that the plaintiff recover of the defendant his costs in this behalf expended; and that the cause be remanded to the court from whence it came for new proceedings to be had, to commence with amending the declaration, which is ordered to be certified to the said court.

Judgment Reversed.
