Primary sources for **Valentine Ball** (1706â€“1770) include his marriage record to **Susannah Lewis** on January 31, 1733, in **Christ Church Parish, Middlesex County, Virginia**. His legal life is documented through **Henrico County voter registrations** from 1752, where he voted for **Burgesses** as a landowner, and multiple land deeds in **Henrico and Amherst Counties**. His **Last Will and Testament**, dated January 12, 1769, and probated March 5, 1770, in **Amherst County**, provides the definitive list of his children and identifies his wife as **Susannah**. Regarding his occupation, records from 1753 indicate he was involved in the tobacco industry, specifically being paid for **"rolling and storing tobacco"** from Westham to the Falls. He was also a significant **landowner**, at one time mortgaging 540 acres of his homestead to Glasgow merchants. While family tradition often links him to the family of **George Washington's mother**, genealogical research explicitly states there is **no evidence** to support kinship to the Balls of Lancaster County.

The military service of his close relatives spans the **Revolutionary War** and the **War of 1812**:

### **Uncles and Senior Kin**
*   **Colonel William Chapman Whitley (Uncle-in-law to grandsons):** A renowned **Indian fighter** and Revolutionary veteran, he later served as a private in the **War of 1812** and was killed at the **Battle of the Thames**.
*   **Joseph Francis Lewis (Father-in-law to grandson Valentine Hargrove):** He was appointed **Lieutenant Colonel** in the **Kentucky Militia** in 1792 and later served as a private in the **7th Regiment, Kentucky Militia**, during the War of 1812.
*   **Thomas Gresham (Kinsman):** Enlisted in the **Continental Army** at age 15 in 1776, serving in **Capt. Rob't Powell's Co, 3rd VA Regt**, and survived the winter at **Valley Forge** under George Washington.

### **Grandsons (Children of Mary Ball and John W. Hargrove Sr.)**
*   **Valentine Hargrove:** A veteran of the **War of 1812**, he served as an **Adjutant** in **Burrus' 16th Regiment of Mississippi Militia**. He was noted in the **Draper Manuscripts** for his bravery while accompanying his uncle-in-law, Col. William Whitley, to **Cherokee towns** to rescue prisoners.
*   **Captain William Hargrove:** A prominent **Indian fighter**, he commanded the **Indiana Rangers** from 1807 to 1812 and served as a **Captain** under William Henry Harrison at the **Battle of Tippecanoe**. In 1814, he was promoted to **Major** of the **4th Regiment of Illinois Mounted Volunteers**.
*   **John "Jack" Gresham (Kinsman/Grandson-in-law):** Served as a private in the **2nd (Williamson) Regt, Mounted Gunman, Tennessee Volunteers**, and fought at the **Battle of New Orleans**.

### **Nephews and Great-Grandsons**
*   **Samuel and Seth Hargrave:** Served as **Lieutenants** in the **4th Regiment of Illinois Mounted Volunteers** in 1814 under their father, Willis Hargrave.
*   **George B. Hargrave:** Served as a **Captain** in the **4th Regiment of Illinois Militia** in 1814.
*   **James E. Hargrave:** Served as a private in **Ewing's Company** during the **War of 1812**.
*   **John Robert Hargrave:** A **Revolutionary War veteran** who also served in the **War of 1812** at the age of 57.
