The Hargrove Study
Ruth Whitley Lewis
Handwritten Note
Ruth Whitley Lewis was the wife of Valentine Hargrove. Her father was Joseph Francis Lewis. Her grand father was Joseph Lewis of Goochland who married Winney King. (This information discovered recently in a Microfilm rented from A.G.L.L., F3652 "Annals of Henrico Parish.")

The Hargrove Study
George Hargrove
Handwritten Note
George Hrgrove patented 250 acres of land in Christianson Co. Ky. on the state waters of the Fort Donelelson Creek on Sept. 12, 1816. ( cert. %2427, found in the archives of Frankfort Ky.) On May 22, 1821 Gorge Hargrove gave his son, William B. Hargrove "For and in consideration of the love and goodwill he doth bear toward the said, William B. Hargrove 100 acres being part of a patent granted to the said George Hargrove... in the year of our Lord One Thousand Eight Hundred and Sixteen." (This found in deed Book A. pg. 120, Trigg Co., Ky)

Handwritten Note
George Hargrove was dead by 20 December 1823 when his heirs sold his 100 acres on Donelson Creek to Joseph Isbell ( Triggs Co. Ky. Deed Book A, pgs. 416,417) From this deed we know he had three sons: William B. Hargrove, Willis Hargrove, and John Hargrove.

Handwritten Note
William B, Hargrove m. Sarah (Sally) but from Court Order Book A. pg.349, Trigg Co. Ky., we have proof of William B. Hargrove's widow, Sarah (Sally) putting out for apprenticeship these children, "herein of William B. Hargrove, deceased." This Court Order was dated 21 Sept. 1829. The Children listed were: Jett (Jethro), Nathan, Nathaniel, and Alfred.
