## Middlesex County, Virginia

### Curator's Notes

Valentine Mayo was born c.1665, place unknown, and died in 1716 in Middlesex County, Virginia. His parentage is unproven. DNA testing proves that he was not related to William Mayo of Isle of Wight, Virginia. Many family researchers believe that he is the son of James Mayo and wife Mary, who settled in Isle of Wight. This is unlikely, as that James Mayo is too young to be Valentine's father. Many believe that his mother's maiden name was Valentine and that he was named for his mother's family.

Valentine appears in Virginia records beginning in the late 1600s. He was claimed as a headright for a William Clapham of Essex County. He witnessed a will in 1686, which helps to prove his date of birth. In 1702 he purchased 39 acres in Middlesex County.

Valentine married Ann Mickelborrough in 1710. She was the daughter of Edmund Mickelborrough and wife Elizabeth Minor. Ann had been married and widowed twice before she married Valentine. They had three known children. A fourth child, an infant who died before his first birthday, is sometimes attributed to this couple.

Valentine died in Middlesex County, Virginia, and was buried in Christ Church Parish. He left no will. His widow Ann was named administratrix of his estate. Documents still exist today for the inventory of his estate.

Many thanks to all the fellow researchers who added notes to this overview. I have left them intact below my notes. Those notes have been very helpful.

## Research Notes

### DNA Evidence and Origins

Valentine Mayo is an immigrant to America. He appears in two land grants as a headright in Essex County, Virginia. The parentage is unproven. James Mayo of Isle of Wight, Virginia is inconsistent in age and location to be his father. yDNA testing at familytreedna.com shows that the descendants of Valentine Mayo of Middlesex, Virginia are not related to the descendants of William Mayo of Isle of Wight, Virginia.

### Virginia Records

Valentine Mayo appeared in Virginia records in the late 1600s. According to Essex County and Middlesex County, Virginia records, Valentine was witness to a will on May 7, 1686. Valentine is also mentioned in the following land grants as an importee to the country:

**Virginia County Court Records - Record Abstracts of Essex County, Virginia** (The Antient Press, 1991, page 30):

Essex County Court, 10th of February 1692/3, p52:

> On margin: Certificate according to Act of Assembly is granted to WM: CLAPHAM for Nineteen hundred acres of land due for ye Importacon of Thirty eight persons into this Colony whose names are VALLINTINE MAYO; [Additional list of names]

**Cavaliers and Pioneers: Abstracts of Virginia Land Patents and Grants, Volume 2, 1666-1695** (Nugent, 1934, page 382):

> MR. ARTHUR SPICER, 2750 acs in the freshes of Rappa. Co., now Essex Co: S. side the river, about 2 mi. up Pwomasend Cr., 29 Apr. 1693, p. 271. Beg. on E. side of a run; over several paths, to Nanzatico; by William White; to Mr. Robert Payne; neare maine run of Portobaga Cr., &c. Granted Alexander Fleming, 17 Apr. 1667 deserted, & now granted by order, &c. Imp. of 55 pers.: Valentine May, [List of names, same 38 names from Essex County Clapham Grant, plus an additional 17]

### Family Information

**The parentage of Valentine Mayo has not been established.**

Valentine married Anne Jordan, a widow, in 1710. Their children include:

1. **James Mayo** (born 1711 in Middlesex County, Virginia) who married (first) Ruth and (second) Martha Williamson
2. **Elizabeth A. Mayo** (born 1713) who married Richard George
3. **Sarah Mayo** (born 1714/15)
4. Possibly **John Mayo** (born 1715)

Valentine Mayo died October 5, 1716 in Middlesex County, Virginia.

## Alternative Family Theory

Some researchers propose that Valentine Mayo's mother was Sarah Mayo, who married Jonathan Brooks. This theory suggests:

- **James Valentine** was born about 1610 in England and immigrated to Isle of Wight, Virginia about 1635
- He may be the father of Jane Valentine (born about 1637) who married Peter Ware, Jr. (they had Valentine Ware, born about 1660)
- Also possibly Mary Valentine (born about 1645) who married James Mayo (they had Valentine Mayo b.1666 Middlesex County, Virginia)
- The Mayo family is known to have emigrated from England to Isle of Wight County, to Middlesex County, Virginia to Caroline County, Virginia, to Spartanburg County, South Carolina

**Note:** Mary Valentine was born approximately 1652 and married James Mayo. They had a son, Valentine Mayo and at least one daughter, Sarah (Sarey) Mayo who married Jonathan Brooks, Sr. James Mayo (1650, Isle of Wight) was the son of William Mayo (abt 1630 of England) and Isobel Hardy.
