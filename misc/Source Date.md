Transcription:

195 Copd Pennary Term 1830 Ordered that the law books in the pos session of Dency white belonging to the Common --- be returned to this Court & by the Clerk to Hower Lawrence Esqr Successor to John White Esqr Decd --- . to the records and papers Ordered that on Smithart --- V Jones Appeal Cont by Appellant Ordered that Court adjourn until 10 Oclock tomorrow we Cunning --- Court met agreeable to adjournment presen James K Gallian Joseph Duncan & Master D Esq Appeal . Continue Jean Clemmon , Plff Peter Parkin , Deft ⎬ --- --- Darby Smithart Wife is Isam Jones Deft ⎬ . Judgfor of the parties and hath for des by agree to be taxed Joseph Duncan absent Jany Wilson and Joseph Duncan Came into Court and took upon themselves the administration of the good and Chattles of the estate of David Wilson Decd Whereupon they entered into Bond with James Sulivan John W Sulivan & Andring as there securities conditioned according to Law Ordered by the Court that Frank Bollinger James H Gilliam Curtis Blakely I Thomas Foley be appointed appraisers of the estate of David of Willson Dec and that a report thereof be returned to our next County Court Ordered that John Speed Smith be appointed guardian for John S. S. Wilson and James Sulwan be appointed guardian for Frances Adeline & Polly infant heirs of David Wilson Ten Term 2nd day 1830 Ordered that Court adjourn untill Court Courer James H. Gallian 196 February Term 1830 7th day County Court held for Whitley County on Monday the day of February 1830 . Present William B Creekmore John Pemberton Dane Duncan McFarlan produced a Commission appointing him Justice of the peace for Whitley County & having taken the oath as required by the Constitution & Laws of Kentucky took his seat - = John Craig appeared in Court and was appointed deporty Clerk who thereupon took the Oath of Office as required by law The appraise of the estate of David Willson Deed together with the bill of sale has been presented to the Court which is ordered to be recorded Sear on returned into Court in the County Lea of William Lay which is deponted with the Clerk John Bracher came into Court and took upon himself the Burthen of the administrator of the good and Chattles of Forton Smith Decd who entered into bond with Joseph I Faris & Joseph Duncan his security as the law directs Ordered that Frank Ballinger all of the County Court be directed to settle with compromise or adjust his suit or other wise the claim which this county court suppose themselves to have on the undertakers of the court house in this County and make report at the Nov. Tenn of this Court
