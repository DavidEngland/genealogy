== Biography ==

Tilman England ...  <ref>Entered by David England, Thursday, August 29, 2013.</ref>

He was born and lived in Bedford County, Tennessee until the 1840's (after 1844, but before 1847 according to where children were born), then he moved to a river farm just west of Florence, Lauderdale Co., AL.  Then it was called ''Cave Springs'', today it is called Smithsonia.  

Born 20 May 1819 in Bedford County, Tennessee; son of John England (age 31) and Elizabeth Roberts (age 26).<ref>Roberts family bible record (John Roberts and Sarah Smith bible), c. 1813–1826.</ref>
Married Eliza Jane (“Liza”); they had at least seven sons and four daughters, based on 1850, 1860, and 1880 census enumerations.<ref>"United States Census, 1850," FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/MH59-TNB); "United States Census, 1860," FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/MHDP-9ZV); "United States Census, 1880," FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/M4JP-H9L).</ref>
Residence recorded in Lauderdale County, Alabama, in the 1866 Alabama State Census.<ref>Alabama State Census, 1866, Lauderdale County — Tilman England household; Soldiers and Widows/Orphans schedule (FamilySearch index).</ref>
Died 2 November 1897 in Florence, Lauderdale County, Alabama; buried at Wesley Chapel United Methodist Church Cemetery.<ref>''The Florence Times'', 11/5/1897; Find A Grave memorial 105127774.</ref>

=== Family ===

In the '''1850 United States Census''', Tilman England is listed as '''head of household''' in '''Lauderdale County, Alabama''', age 31, with wife '''Eliza''', age 34.<ref>"United States Census, 1850," index and images, FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/MH59-TNB), Tilmon England, 1850.</ref>

The household included:
* '''John''' England (age 11, born Tennessee)
* '''Matilda''' England (age 10, born Tennessee)
* '''Martha''' England (age 8, born Tennessee)
* '''George''' England (age 6, born Tennessee)
* '''James''' England (age 6, born Alabama)
* '''Margaret''' England (age 10–11 months, born Alabama)

=== 1860 United States Census ===

In 1860, Tilman (age 43, b. Tennessee) was enumerated in District 1, Lauderdale County, Alabama; race White, sex Male. Household members listed were: '''Eliza Jane England''', '''Malisa England''', '''John England''', '''Martha F England''', '''George D England''', '''James M England''', '''Margaret C England''', '''Richard A England''', and '''Benjamen England'''.<ref>"United States Census, 1860," FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1/MHDP-9ZV), District 1, Lauderdale, Alabama.</ref>

=== 1866 Alabama State Census ===

Lauderdale County household counts:
- '''Males:''' 1 under 10; 4 aged 10–20; 1 aged 50–60
- '''Females:''' 1 aged 10–20; 1 aged 20–30; 1 aged 50–60 (Eliza)

The soldiers schedule notes '''John England''', soldier, died of sickness.<ref>Alabama State Census, 1866, Lauderdale County — Tilman England household; Soldiers and Widows/Orphans schedule (FamilySearch index).</ref>
