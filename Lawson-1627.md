== Biography ==
James Alvin "Jim" Lawson was born on 6 October 1868 in Wayne County, Tennessee, the son of George Washington Lawson and Sara Jane Brewer.<ref>"United States, Census, 1870", FamilySearch (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MD8Z-P4S : Sat Jan 18 22:49:10 UTC 2025), Entry for G W Lawson and Sarah J Lawson, 1870.</ref><ref>"United States, Census, 1880", FamilySearch (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MDWV-ZDR : Sun Jan 19 21:53:24 UTC 2025), Entry for George W. Lawson and Sarah J. Lawson, 1880.</ref>

He married Mary Ella Horton on 15 July 1894 in Wayne County, Tennessee. They had nine children.<ref>"Tennessee, County Marriages, 1790-1959", FamilySearch (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:QKH3-63VT : Sat Mar 09 19:30:31 UTC 2024), Entry for James A Lawson and Mary E Horton, 15 Jul 1894.</ref>

In 1900, James lived in Civil District 8, Wayne County, Tennessee.<ref>"United States, Census, 1900", FamilySearch (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MSZV-74D : Fri Oct 17 11:49:01 UTC 2025), Entry for James * Lawson and Mary E Lawson, 1900.</ref> He later lived in Wolf, Seminole County, Oklahoma for about 10 years.<ref>"United States, Census, 1910", FamilySearch (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MLQ7-RNN : Wed Aug 13 18:59:52 UTC 2025), Entry for James A Lauson and Mary E Lauson, 1910.</ref><ref>"United States, Census, 1920", FamilySearch (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MJMG-C17 : Thu Jan 09 05:10:35 UTC 2025), Entry for J A Lawson and Mary Eller Lawson, 1920.</ref>

James died on 2 August 1951 in Oklahoma at age 82 and was buried in Seminole, Seminole County, Oklahoma.<ref>"Find a Grave Index", FamilySearch (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:QV2C-75Q5 : Wed Apr 02 03:17:59 UTC 2025), Entry for James A. Lawson.</ref>
