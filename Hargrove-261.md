== Biography ==

John Hargrove served as a Minuteman from Amherst Co., VA under Capt. Nicholas Cabell from 18 March 1776 until October 1776. He was ordered into the service of the Continental Army on 21 June 1781 by Daniel Gaines, Colonel of the Militia and marched to join the army commanded by the Honorable Major General Marquis de la Fayette. He received a Treasury Land Warrant for 2000 acres of land in present day Kentucky. Documentation to prove his service can be found in the book, "The Amherst County Story" by Alfred Percy on pages 40--51 (note page 47); On page 70 of the Index to the book, "Soldiers in the Revolution from Amherst, County, Virginia". The deed for his land warrant is noted on page 106 of Jillison's book entitled, "Old Kentucky Entries and Deeds". Also, there is a book written by Davis, page 183, entitled, "Deeds of Amherst County, VA 1761--1807".

John Hargrove's wife was Mary Ball. Her parents were Valentine Ball and Susannah Lewis. Valentine Ball was born circa 1713-15 in Amherst Co., V A and died before 5 Feb. 1770 in the same county. He married Susannah Ball on 31 Jan. 1733 in Middlesex Co., VA. His will is dated 5 Feb. 1770 and is recorded in Amherst Co. He was the father of 10 children.
