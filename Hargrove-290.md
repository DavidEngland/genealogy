== Biography ==

'''John Lindsey Hargrove''' was born on '''January 24, 1826''', in '''Alabama''', United States.<ref name="birth1826">"United States Census, 1860", database with images, FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MHZY-RPP : accessed 26 Jul 2017), John L Hargrove, 1860.</ref> He was the son of '''[[Hargrove-277|Valentine Hargrove]]''' (c. 1778–1858) and '''[[Lewis-8883|Ruth Whitley Lewis]]''' (1791–). At the time of his birth, his father was 49 years old and his mother was 46.

=== Sibling Relationship ===

John Lindsey Hargrove and his brother '''[[Hargrove-277|Valentine Hargrove]]''' maintained an exceptionally close relationship throughout their lives, characteristic of the tight-knit Hargrove family network during the frontier era. Both brothers were instrumental in the westward expansion of the family, with Valentine establishing roots in Tennessee and John eventually settling in Texas. Their close bond was reinforced by their shared experiences as sons of a prominent Kentucky-Tennessee frontier figure and their roles as patriarchs of expanding family branches.

=== Western Migration and Residences ===

John Lindsey Hargrove was part of the mid-19th-century pioneer wave that pushed the American frontier westward. His family's relocation to '''Texas''' reflected the broader pattern of settlement expansion following the Civil War era. Census records document his presence in:

* 1860 – Alabama
* 1880 – Justice Precinct 3, Palo Pinto, Texas<ref name="census1880" />
* 1900 – Mitchell, Texas, United States<ref name="census1900">"United States Census, 1900", database with images, FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:M3P2-P6H : accessed 10 Oct 2017), John L Hargrove, Mitchell, Texas.</ref>

=== Death and Burial ===

John Lindsey Hargrove died on '''May 21, 1902''', in '''Texas''', United States, at the age of 76 years.<ref name="burial">Find A Grave Index, database, FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:QVKM-ZDZW : accessed 13 Dec 2015), John Lindsey Hargrove, 1902; Burial, Mitchell Land District, Texas.</ref> He was buried in '''Mitchell Land District, Texas''', marking the final resting place of a man whose life spanned the transformation of the American South and West during the nineteenth century.
