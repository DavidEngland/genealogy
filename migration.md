== Migration Patterns: Virginia → Kentucky → Alabama → Tennessee ==

This page documents the interconnected migration patterns of the Lewis, Hargrove, White, and McDonald families through the American frontier regions of Virginia, Kentucky, Alabama, and Tennessee from the late 18th through 19th centuries.

== Ruth Whitley Lewis ==

'''Ruth Whitley Lewis''' (c. 1780 Lincoln, Kentucky – before 1840 Hardin County, Tennessee)

* '''Parents:''' Joseph Francis Lewis (brickmason and militia leader) and Sarah Whitley [1, 2]
* '''Marriage:''' Valentine Hargrove, 9 Oct 1799, Lincoln, Kentucky [2, 3]
* '''Death:''' Hardin County, Tennessee, before 1840 [1]

'''Children:'''
* James Monroe Hargrove (1818–1884) [3, 4]

'''Migration:''' Kentucky (birth, marriage) → Tennessee (death)

== Hargrove Family ==

'''James Monroe Hargrove''' (1818–1884)

* '''Parents:''' Valentine Hargrove and Ruth Whitley Lewis
* '''Children:''' Lydia Serena Hargrove (b. 1854 Hardin County, Tennessee) [5]

'''Migration:''' Tennessee and Alabama border regions [3, 4]

== Dempsey White ==

'''Dempsey E. White''' (before 1798 – c. 1862)

* '''Occupation:''' Farmer; acquired farms and transferred them to sons [4, 6]
* '''Children:''' James Ervin White (1838 Kentucky – 1897) [5]

== White Family ==

'''James Ervin White''' (1838 Kentucky – 1897)

* '''Parents:''' Dempsey E. White
* '''Marriage:''' Lydia Serena Hargrove (daughter of James Monroe Hargrove) [5]

'''Migration:''' Kentucky (birth) → Tennessee (marriage, death)

== McDonald Family ==

'''Migration Pattern:''' South Carolina → Tennessee (Bedford and Lawrence Counties) → Lauderdale County, Alabama (1834) → Tennessee [10-12]

* '''John McDonald:''' Sold 240-acre Alabama farm in 1854; returned to Wayland Springs, Tennessee [13]
* '''Jahue Scruggs McDonald:''' Leased railroad car to move family, furniture, and possessions by rail to Oklahoma in 1918 following bad crop year in Texas [15]

'''Tragic Events:''' Three of John Scruggs McDonald's children died in railroad/trestle accidents in Alabama and Tennessee [16]

== Lewis and Gresham Families ==

'''Origin:''' Virginia [7-9]

'''Migration:''' Virginia → Kentucky frontier → Alabama/Tennessee

== Economic and Transportation Factors ==

'''Causes of Migration:'''
* Land availability and speculation [14]
* Economic cycles [10-12]
* Rising land prices in settled areas [14]

'''Railroad Era:'''
* Emory/Embry brothers: Early railroad investors for Tennessee iron ore mines to Florence, Alabama route [17, 18]
* Rail transportation enabled large-scale family migrations (1918 example) [15]
* Railroad accidents claimed multiple family members [16]
