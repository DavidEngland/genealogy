== Biography ==

'''Mary Jane Scott''' (23 December 1842 – 24 September 1907) was born in '''Wayne County, Tennessee''', to '''John Martin Scott''' (1791–1870) and '''Mary Cynthia White Scott'''. She lived during a transformative period in Tennessee frontier history and became the mother of at least thirteen children through her marriage to '''William T. Pigg'''.

=== Early Life and Family Origins ===

Mary Jane was born on '''23 December 1842''' in '''Wayne County, Tennessee'''. She was the daughter of '''John Martin Scott''' (b. 1791), a '''Magistrate in Wayne County''', and '''Mary Cynthia White Scott'''. Mary Jane was the fourth of at least nine children born to this union.

Her father, '''John Martin Scott''', had migrated to Wayne County from North Carolina prior to 1820, settling on '''48 Mile Creek''' on the east side of Wayne County. He was the son of '''James Scott''' (b. 1765, North Carolina) and '''Rebecca Scott'''. John Martin Scott filed for a '''Revolutionary War Tract of 200 acres''', indicating his family's early settlement status in the region.

Mary Jane's mother, '''Mary Cynthia White''', was the daughter of '''George White''', described as an "early settler in Wayne County" who owned a farm a few hundred yards southwest of J.N. Davis's land. The White family was among the pioneering families of the region, which included the Coopers, Sims, Lindsey, Robersons, Nowlins, Gallaher, Parkers, Dickerson, Davis, Thompson (who owned Martins Mills), Yates, and Walkers.

=== Death and Legacy ===

Mary Jane died on '''24 September 1907''' at approximately 65 years of age. She was buried in '''Lawson Cemetery''', Wayne County, Tennessee. Her legacy continues through her thirteen children and numerous grandchildren who remained in the Wayne County region.

=== Historical Context ===

Mary Jane lived through one of the most turbulent periods in American history:
* '''1842 (birth):''' Antebellum Tennessee frontier
* '''1861 (marriage):''' Outbreak of the American Civil War
* '''1861–1865:''' Civil War period when Tennessee families were divided between Union and Confederate sympathies
* '''1865–1907:''' Reconstruction and post-bellum Tennessee

The Scott and Pigg families were characteristic of Wayne County settlers who remained in place through these transformative decades, maintaining agricultural pursuits in the region.

=== Related Families and Connections ===

'''The Scott Family:''' A prominent Wayne County family with roots in North Carolina. The family name appears frequently in Wayne County records from 1820 onwards, with multiple individuals named James, John, and variations throughout the 19th century.

'''The White Family:''' Through her mother, Mary Jane was connected to the White pioneer families of Wayne County. Her maternal grandfather, George White, was an early settler in the region.

'''The Pigg Family:''' Through her marriage, Mary Jane became part of the Pigg family network, which became established landholders and farmers in Wayne County.

Further research needed to clarify if she died in Alabama or if these are cross-indexed records.
* '''Burial:''' Lawson Cemetery, Wayne County, Tennessee (confirmed in multiple Find a Grave Index entries)
* '''Father's Magistrate Service:''' John Martin Scott served as a Magistrate in Wayne County
* '''Family Records:''' Some genealogical sources list multiple marriages for John Martin Scott and variations in family member names; further research is needed to confirm exact relationships
* '''Census Records:''' Mary Jane appears in census records:
  - 1850 Census (multiple entries with variations in name spelling)
