== Biography ==

'''Jarred Gresham''' (also spelled '''Grisham''') was born in '''1801''' in '''Wilkes County, Georgia''', the son of [[Gresham-188|Thomas Gresham]] and [[Thornton-XXX|Mary Thornton]].<ref>Gresham family genealogy; Wilkes County, Georgia records</ref> While family tradition may identify him as the youngest son, documented lists of Thomas's ten children include several siblings born after Jarred, such as Benjamin C., James, and William B.

=== Migration and Settlement ===

Following the family's migration from Georgia, Jarred lived for several years in '''Tennessee''', specifically in '''Giles and Haywood Counties'''.<ref>United States Census, 1850 and 1860; Tennessee county records</ref> He eventually relocated to '''Lauderdale County, Alabama''', arriving in '''March 1856'''.<ref>Lauderdale County, Alabama property and administrative records, March 1856</ref> He established himself as a '''farmer''' in the region, and by the 1850 census, his estate was valued at '''$300.00'''.<ref>"United States Census, 1850," index and images, FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/MH5S-SW8 : accessed 08 Sep 2013), Jarred Greasam, 1850.</ref>

=== Marriage and Family ===

Jarred married '''Susannah Ann Morrison''' (the daughter of [[Morrison-XXX|Peter Morrison]] and [[Morrison-XXX|Ann Morrison]]) around '''1830'''.<ref>Lauderdale County, Alabama marriage records; family genealogy</ref> Together, they were the parents of several children, including [[Gresham-183|William George Gresham]], who carried on the family's religious legacy as a '''Church of Christ minister''' after serving in the Civil War.

=== Community Service ===

In addition to his farming activities, Jarred played an administrative role in the Lauderdale County community. He '''administered Peter Morrison's will''' in '''March 1856''', demonstrating his standing as a trusted member of the local community.<ref>"Lauderdale County, Alabama Probate Records," March 1856; administrative documents on file</ref>

=== Death and Legacy ===

Jarred died '''circa 1865''' in '''Lauderdale County, Alabama'''. Both he and his wife Susannah Ann are buried in the '''Pleasant Hill Cemetery''' in Lauderdale County, located near Florence.<ref>Find a Grave, database and images (https://www.findagrave.com : accessed [DATE]), Pleasant Hill Cemetery, Lauderdale County, Alabama</ref> Although his specific grave site has not been located in modern times, his presence in census and property records confirms his role as a foundational member of the '''Gresham''' line in the '''Shoals area'''.
