Prominent Members of the 16th Regiment
Valentine Hargrove (Adjutant)
* Rank: Adjutant.
* WikiTree ID: Hargrove-277.
* Details: Born in Amherst, VA, he was a renowned "good warrior" who served alongside his uncle-in-law, Col. William Whitley. He was a pivotal figure in the transition of North Alabama from illegal "intruder" status to established settlement. He eventually settled in Hardin County, TN.
James Hartgrove (Hargrove) (Private)
* Rank: Private.
* WikiTree ID: Hargrove-275.
* Details: Recorded in the roster as "James Hartgrove". He was the brother of Valentine and Lindsey Hargrove. He remained in the Kentucky/Tennessee borderlands, receiving land deeds from his brother Valentine in 1828.

Charles Burrus (Lieutenant-Colonel Commandant)
* Rank: Lieutenant-Colonel.
* Details: Appointed commandant in 1812, he was a member of the local elite in Madison County. His family was deeply integrated into the social structure of early Huntsville; his sister married the merchant John J. Fackler.
Aaron Gibson (Sergeant)
* Rank: Sergeant.
* Details: Gibson was a quintessential "settler-soldier." He was identified as an "intruder" on Chickasaw lands as early as 1807 in the Elk River area and was a lead petitioner to President Madison in 1810 to remain on the land. His military service in 1813 facilitated his transition to a recognized citizen of the territory.
Amos and Benjamin French (Privates)
* Rank: Privates.
* WikiTree IDs: [French-2157] (Amos) and [French-144] (Benjamin).
* Details: Both were early Madison County pioneers and petitioners for land rights in 1810. Benjamin French's service in the 16th Regiment was part of his lifelong engagement in the development of Madison and Limestone Counties.
