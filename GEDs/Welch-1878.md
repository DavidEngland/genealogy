== Biography ==

'''Serena Welch''' (abt. 1833–1884) married '''James Monroe Hargrove''' (1818–1884) in Hardin County, Tennessee, becoming the second wife following the death of his first wife, Lydia Poole. Serena’s household was centered along the Tennessee–Alabama border, with ties to the Whitley/Lewis frontier families through her husband’s parents, Valentine Hargrove and Ruth Whitley Lewis.

Serena appears in the 1850 census in her father Elijah’s household in Hardin County, Tennessee.<ref>"United States Census, 1850," database with images, ''FamilySearch'' (https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MCDN-FW7 : accessed 05 Sep 2013), Serena Welch in entry for Elijah C Welsh, Hardin County, Tennessee.</ref> By 1860 and 1870, she is enumerated in the household of her husband James Monroe Hargrove in Hardin County.<ref>"United States Census, 1860," database with images, ''FamilySearch'' (https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:M8TD-MLN : accessed 26 Jul 2017), James M Hargroves, Hardin County, Tennessee.</ref><ref>"United States Census, 1870," database with images, ''FamilySearch'' (https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MDD7-3B6 : accessed 12 Apr 2016), James Hargroves, Hardin County, Tennessee; citing NARA microfilm publication M593.</ref>

=== Life and Legacy ===
Serena lived through the Civil War era on the TN–AL line. The family remained in Hardin County, and elder Valentine Hargrove resided with them in later years. Serena died in 1884.
