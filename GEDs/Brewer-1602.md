== Biography ==

'''Sarah Jane Brewer''' (February 17, 1847 – March 13, 1923) was a matriarch of the Lawson family, deeply rooted in the pioneer history of the Tennessee–Alabama borderlands.<ref name="FAIndex">FamilySearch, Find A Grave Index, entry "Sarah Jane Brewer Lawson", https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:QVPQ-PD6N.</ref>

=== Early Life and Family Origins ===

Sarah Jane was born on '''February 17, 1847''', in '''Wayne County, Tennessee''', to '''Ryal Brewer''' (age 30) and '''Hannah Horton''' (age 18).<ref name="FS1850">FamilySearch, United States Census, 1850, Wayne County, Tennessee, household of Riley (Ryal) Brewer and Hannah (includes Sarah), https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:MC63-H3H.</ref><ref name="FS1860">FamilySearch, United States Census, 1860, Wayne County, Tennessee, household of R. Brewer and Hannah (includes Sarah), https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:M8TY-J25.</ref> She appeared in her parents' household in both the 1850 and 1860 Wayne County censuses, confirming her presence in the region through her formative years.

=== Marriage and Family Life ===

Sarah Jane married '''George Washington Lawson''' (1841–1925) on '''July 26, 1866''', in '''Wayne County, Tennessee'''.<ref name="FSMarriage">FamilySearch, Tennessee, County Marriages, 1790–1959, Wayne County, entry for George W. Lawson and Sarah Brewer (26 July 1866), https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:Q2Z9-XJXP.</ref> This union linked her to long-standing families in Wayne County, Tennessee, and Lauderdale County, Alabama.

Sarah and George were the parents of at least '''ten children—eight sons and two daughters'''—identified through census records and death certificates.<ref name="FS1850" /><ref name="FS1860" /><ref name="FSThomasDeath">FamilySearch, Tennessee Deaths, 1914–1966, death of Thomas W. Lawson (parents: Geo. Lawson and Sarah Brewer), https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:NS2Q-HF5.</ref><ref name="FSTexasMelissa">FamilySearch, Texas Deaths, 1890–1977, death of Melissa Bridges (parents: George W Lawson and Sarah Brewer), https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:K7DQ-L9B.</ref>

=== Later Life and Residences ===

In '''1910''', Sarah lived in '''Martins Mills, Wayne County, Tennessee'''.<ref name="Census1910">1910 U.S. Census, Wayne County, Tennessee, Martins Mills (citation to be added upon image review).</ref> 

By '''1920''', she had relocated to '''Civil District 4, Wayne County, Tennessee'''.<ref name="Census1920">1920 U.S. Census, Wayne County, Tennessee, Civil District 4 (citation to be added upon image review).</ref> This move, late in her life, kept her within the region where she had spent the majority of her years.

=== Death and Burial ===

Sarah Jane Brewer Lawson died on '''March 13, 1923''', in '''Wayne County, Tennessee''', at the age of 76. She is buried in '''Cromwell Crossroads Cemetery''' in '''Collinwood, Wayne County, Tennessee''', alongside her husband '''George Washington Lawson''' (1841–1925).<ref name="FA">{{FindAGrave|135928719 }}</ref>

=== Legacy and Family Connections ===

Within the broader family tree, Sarah Jane is a foundational genealogical link: she is the '''paternal grandmother of Ann Lawson''' and the '''2nd-great-grandmother of family historian David England'''.<ref name="GED">England family records and compiled genealogy; WikiTree import "annie lawson ancestors.ged" (2013).</ref> Her likeness appears in '''early Wayne County photographs''' preserved in the [[England-1357|England family collection]].<ref name="Photos">Wayne County archival photographs, England family collection.</ref>

The descendants and connections of Sarah Jane Brewer Lawson continue to maintain ties to Wayne County and the surrounding region, preserving the memory of this matriarch who witnessed and participated in the transformation of the Tennessee frontier.
