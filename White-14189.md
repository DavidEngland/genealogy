== Biography ==

'''Dempsey E. White''' was born about '''1798''' in '''North Carolina''' (or '''Kentucky'''), United States. He was the son of '''[[White-12146|John Calvin White Sr.]]''' and '''[[Shelton-1046|Mary (Shelton) White]]'''. He had half-siblings Richard, Hendricks, George, Charlotta, Harrison, Mary, John Calvin Jr., and Nicholas A. White (per Whitley County, Kentucky records).

=== Marriage ===

He married '''[[Perkins-3681|Nancy Perkins]]''' on '''24 January 1819''' in '''Whitley County, Kentucky'''. Nancy was the daughter of '''[[Perkins-1694|Jabez Perkins]]''' of Connecticut.

=== Civic Service ===

Dempsey served as a '''member of the Kentucky Legislature''', reflecting the family's prominent status in frontier society. He was also a prosperous landowner and traveled extensively.

=== Children ===

Dempsey and Nancy had at least nine children together:

* '''[[White-14308|Jabes White]]''' (b. 1818)
* '''[[White-14277|Jesse Perkins White Sr.]]''' (b. 1820)
* '''[[White-14276|Milton E. White]]''' (b. 1824)
* '''[[White-14310|Dempsy Lawson White M.D.]]''' (1826–1891)
* '''[[White-14309|Emily (White) Mahan]]'''
* '''[[White-14307|Louisa J. White]]'''
* '''[[White-14147|James Ervin "Ervie" White]]''' (1838–1897)
* '''[[White-14274|Nancy (White) Parker]]'''
* '''[[White-14311|Mary A (White) Lawson]]'''

=== Migration ===

About '''1843''', Dempsey and Nancy relocated from Whitley County, Kentucky to '''Hardin County, Tennessee'''. Census records from '''1850 and 1860''' enumerate the family in Tennessee, indicating permanent settlement in the region.

=== Death ===

Dempsey E. White died about '''1859''' in '''Hardin County, Tennessee'''.
