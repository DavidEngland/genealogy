== Biography ==

**[Palina Altimira McDonald](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/McDonald-4057)**, affectionately known as **"Kitty,"** was born on **March 31, 1859**, at **Wayland Springs, Lawrence County, Tennessee**. She was the fourth of ten children born to **[Thomas Anderson McDonald](https://www.wikitree.com/index.php?title=Special:SearchPerson&wpFirstName=Thomas%20Anderson&wpLastName=McDonald&wpBirthDate=1822)**, a blacksmith, and **[Sarah A. Adkinson](https://www.wikitree.com/index.php?title=Special:SearchPerson&wpFirstName=Sarah%20A.&wpLastName=Adkinson)**. Her middle name was given in honor of her aunt, **[Artimena McDonald English](https://www.wikitree.com/index.php?title=Special:SearchPerson&wpFirstName=Artimena&wpLastName=McDonald)**.

### **Early Life and Family Context**
Palina was part of a large family with deep roots in the **Clan Donald**. Her family followed a southern migration pattern, moving from South Carolina into Tennessee and eventually Alabama. During her early childhood, the family lived in **Wayne and Lawrence Counties, Tennessee**, before moving to **Cypress Creek** in Lauderdale County, Alabama, by 1870. There, they lived as neighbors to her uncle, John Scruggs McDonald.

The family history is noted for its **technical craftsmanship**; her father and several uncles were renowned **blacksmiths and gunsmiths** who produced "Kentucky Rifles". Interestingly, family records indicate that in various census reports, Palina’s surname was often recorded as **"McDaniel,"** a common variation for the MacDonald family in that era.

### **Marriage and Children**
On **November 29, 1876**, Palina married **[William M "Bill" England](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1361)**. Her husband was born on March 1, 1853, and passed away on March 6, 1905. Following her husband's death, a 1908 photograph of their family farm near the **Rhodesville Community** shows the family residing in a **story-and-a-half structure** with a full front porch and a picket fence.

Palina and William had **eight children**:
1. **[Ella (England) James](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1364)** (1877–1910), who married [Robert James](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/James-31739).
2. **[William Edward "Ed" England](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1360)** (1879–1964), a farmer and conservationist; married [Nancy Pearl Parish](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Parish-370).
3. **[Tilmon England](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1365)** (1883–1894).
4. **[Neual England](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1366)** (1884–1902).
5. **[Roxie May (England) Hurst](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1075)** (1887–1979), who married [John L. McClure](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/McClure-610) and later Levi Hurst.
6. **[Rivers Walsie England](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1368)** (1893–1971), who married [Nettie Bevis](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Bevis-50).
7. **[Waymond Bessie (England) Burns](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1369)** (1899–1981).
8. **[Carver Colbert England](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/England-1370)** (1902–1978), who married [Daisy Violet Glasscock](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Glasscock-203).

### **Death and Legacy**
Palina passed away on **August 5, 1936**. She is buried at the **Wesley Chapel United Methodist Church** near Cloverdale, Alabama. She is remembered through family stories as being part of a lineage characterized by "temper, resolve, and independence," traits attributed to her **Scots-Celtic ancestry**.
