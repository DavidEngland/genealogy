**Nancy Perkins White** was born in **1803** in **Grayson County, Virginia**. She was the daughter of the frontier pioneer **Jabez (Jabus) Perkins** and his first wife, whose identity is recorded in historical documents as **"Unknown,"** though some researchers unprovenly suggest her name was Nancy Ann Creekmore.

Through her father, Nancy was a descendant of the immigrant **Edward Perkins**, who settled in **New Haven, Connecticut**, in 1637. Her branch of the family eventually migrated to the **North Carolina and Virginia backcountry**, where her grandfather, **Timothy Perkins**, was a known **Loyalist** during the American Revolution.

### **Marriage and Kentucky Life**
Around **1813**, Nancy moved with her family to the **Knox/Whitley County** area of **Kentucky**. On **January 28, 1819**, she married **Dempsey White** in Whitley County. Her husband was a prominent local figure who served as a member of the **Kentucky Legislature**. Together they raised a family that included your ancestor **James Ervin White** (1838–1897).

### **Inheritance and Legacy**
Nancy is a focal point in her father’s final legal records. In his **1835 will**, written while he was "sick and low in boddy," Jabez Perkins specifically looked after his daughters, bequeathing to **Nancy White** and her sister **Amy White** "one Note of Land on **Demcey White** to be equally divided between them".

Interestingly, on the same day he wrote his will, her father married his second wife, who was also named **Nancy White**. Family records indicate that Nancy Perkins White lived a long life on the frontier and passed away sometime after **1860**.
