The sources document a remarkable array of famous historical figures who descend from the foundational families mentioned in your genealogy, particularly the **Warner**, **Lewis**, **Whitley**, and **Gresham** lineages.

### **The Augustine Warner Sr. "Oligarchy"**
**Augustine Warner Sr.** and his wife **Mary Townley** are identified as the progenitors of one of the most influential "ruling oligarchies" in American history. Their direct descendants include some of the most prominent leaders of the English-speaking world:
*   **George Washington:** The first U.S. President was the grandson of **Mildred Warner** and Lawrence Washington.
*   **Meriwether Lewis:** The famed explorer and leader of the **Corps of Discovery** was a direct descendant of **Elizabeth Warner** and John Lewis.
*   **General Robert E. Lee:** The Confederate general was a descendant of **Sarah Warner** and Lawrence Townley.
*   **General George S. Patton:** The World War II general is cited as a direct descendant of the Warner-Townley line.
*   **Queen Elizabeth II:** The sources note that the British monarch shared this same direct Virginian ancestry through the lineage of **Mary Warner** and John Smith.

### **The Whitley and Lewis Frontier Leaders**
The **Whitley family** produced figures who were foundational to the development of the American frontier and the federal government:
*   **William Chapman Whitley:** A legendary Kentucky pioneer and Indian fighter, he is famous for building **"Sportsman's Hill,"** the first brick house west of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
*   **John Aaron Rawlins:** A descendant of **Ruth Whitley** and Moses Collier, Rawlins served as a **Union Army General** and was the **U.S. Secretary of War** under President Ulysses S. Grant.
*   **David Lewis:** Serving from 1872 to 1874, he was the **24th Governor of Alabama**.
*   **Reuben Lewis:** The younger brother of Meriwether Lewis, he was a significant **Missouri River fur trader** and served as a government **Indian Agent** for the Cherokees.

### **Gresham and McDonald Pioneers and Public Figures**
The Gresham and McDonald lineages include individuals who were instrumental in the early settlement and cultural life of the South:
*   **Thomas Gresham:** A Revolutionary War veteran who served at **Valley Forge**, he is recognized as the **first white settler in Lauderdale County, Alabama**.
*   **Stephen Emory McDonald:** A descendant of the North Alabama McDonald line, he served as a **Representative and Congressman** in the Alabama Legislature in the early 20th century.
*   **John Anderson McDonald:** Known as the "voice of agribusiness," he was a **nationally known broadcaster** on **WSM Radio** in Nashville, famous for his daily farm reports.
*   **Milo Green Grisham:** A descendant of the Tennessee Gresham line, he was a prominent lawyer who served as **City Attorney** and City Clerk in Sikeston, Missouri.

### **Historical Significance**
These descendants represent a transition from the **colonial gentry** of Virginia and the **warrior herdsmen** of the Scottish Highlands into the highest levels of American military, political, and cultural leadership. The sources suggest that the "fighting spirit" and "temper, resolve, and independence" of these early families provided the foundational character for these famous American figures.
