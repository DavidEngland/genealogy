Primary documentation and genealogical records confirm that **Sarah Tapley "Sally" Clark** (1806–1894) was the wife of Lauderdale County pioneer **Moses "Mose" Duncan**. 

### **Biographical Summary: Sarah Tapley Clark Duncan**
*   **Full Name:** Sarah Tapley Clark (often recorded as "Sally" or "Aunt Sallie").
*   **Birth:** She was born on **February 23, 1806**, in **Tennessee**.
*   **Marriage:** She married **Moses Duncan** on **March 27, 1829**, in **Lauderdale County, Alabama**. 
*   **Death:** She died at the age of 88 on **June 6, 1894**, in **Lauderdale County, Alabama**.

### **Family and Children**
The 1850 Federal Census for Lauderdale County and subsequent death records for her children provide a clear picture of her household. Her children with Moses Duncan included:
*   **Sarah Duncan** (b. ca. 1830).
*   **George Duncan** (b. ca. 1832).
*   **Martha E. Duncan** (b. July 25, 1832): She married **Jesse P. Whitten** (1827–1882), further connecting the Duncan line to the Whitten and Parish families.
*   **Robert Duncan** (b. ca. 1834).
*   **Mary Duncan** (b. ca. 1836).
*   **Elmira (or Almira) Duncan** (b. ca. 1840).
*   **Thomas J. Duncan** (b. ca. 1842): His Alabama death record explicitly identifies his parents as **Moses Duncan** and **Sarah Clark**.
*   **James Andrew Duncan** (b. ca. 1845): His death record also confirms his parents as **Moses Duncan** and **Sarah Clark**.
